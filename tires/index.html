Signs You Need New Tires

Watch for these warning signs to keep your vehicle safe on the road.

Reduced Tread Depth

Tires should be replaced when tread reaches 2/32 inch. Use the penny test: insert a penny upside down—if you see Lincoln's head, it's time.

Uneven Wear

Uneven wear patterns indicate alignment, inflation, or suspension issues. This reduces tire life and driving safety.

Vibration or Pulling

Vibrations or your vehicle pulling to one side may indicate unbalanced, misaligned, or damaged tires.

Cracks or Bulges

Visible cracks, bulges, or blisters in the sidewall mean the tire is damaged and needs immediate replacement.

Excessive Noise

Loud thumping or unusual tire noise while driving suggests wear or damage—have them inspected immediately.

Age

Even if tread looks good, replace tires every 6–10 years due to rubber degradation, regardless of mileage.

How to Read Your Tire Size

Tire sizes are displayed on the sidewall using a standardized format. Here's how to decode it:

Width

Tire width in millimeters (225mm)

Aspect Ratio

Sidewall height as % of width (65% of 225mm)

Type

R = Radial construction

Wheel Diameter

Wheel diameter in inches (17 inches)

Load Index

Maximum weight capacity per tire

Speed Rating

Maximum safe speed (H = 130 mph)

How to Measure Tread Depth

Keep your tires safe with regular tread depth checks. Here are the most common methods:

The Penny Test

Insert a penny upside down into the tread grooves. If you can see the top of Lincoln's head, your tread is at 2/32 inch—time for replacement.

Tread Depth Gauge

Use a dedicated gauge for precise measurements. Most are inexpensive and provide exact readings in 32nds of an inch.

Visual Inspection

Many modern tires have built-in wear indicators. When treads wear flush with these bars, replacement is needed.

Professional Inspection

Have a technician measure your tread depth during regular service. We offer free inspections at every visit.

Pro Tip: Check tread depth monthly and always maintain proper tire pressure for safety and longevity.
